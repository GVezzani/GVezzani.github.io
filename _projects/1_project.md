## Project Description

This project, part of the [Inclusive Humanities](https://inclusivehumanities.eu/) initiative, explores how online environments are reshaping the way literary value is created and perceived in the digital age. With the rise of social reading platforms, user-generated ratings, and online discussions, the authority of traditional literary criticism is increasingly complemented —and sometimes challenged— by the voices of everyday readers.

By combining approaches from literary studies and digital humanities, the project investigates how different forms of reviewing —ranging from professional critics to amateur audiences— contribute to new models of interpretation and reception. Special attention is given to how these practices influence the construction of cultural value and the evolution of the literary canon.

Ultimately, the project aims to better understand how digital environments are transforming the role of criticism, expanding participation in literary discourse, and redefining what counts as literary significance in contemporary culture.
